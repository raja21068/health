Optimal Age Range for Childbearing

Data Table
Age Range Fertility & Health Considerations Medical Observations
20-24 Highest fertility; lowest risk of complications. Biologically optimal; ideal for minimizing health risks.
25-30 High fertility; good balance of health and stability. Still optimal; many consider this range for family planning.
30-35 Fertility starts to decline gradually; slight increase in risks. Considered healthy but with a slight increase in complications.
35-40 Noticeable decline in fertility; increased risk of complications. Medical guidance recommended; risks of complications are higher.
40+ Sharp decline in fertility; higher need for medical support. High-risk pregnancies; often need fertility treatments.

*Individual health varies; consult a healthcare provider for personalized advice.
