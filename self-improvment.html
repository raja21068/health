Kaizen Goal-Setting Principles

The Incremental Method

Start with small, manageable steps to avoid feeling overwhelmed. This allows for steady progress and builds momentum over time.

Feedback Loops

Continuously assess progress and make adjustments based on feedback. Regular check-ins help refine actions and stay aligned with goals.

The 1% Rule

Focus on making tiny, 1% improvements consistently. Over time, these small changes accumulate and lead to substantial progress.

Visual Tracking

Use visual tools like charts, graphs, or checklists to monitor progress. Visual tracking provides motivation and keeps focus on the end goal.

Reward Systems

Celebrate small achievements along the way. Rewarding milestones encourages continued effort and reinforces positive behavior.

Ask Five Questions

Before starting or when facing challenges, ask yourself these five key questions:

  • Why is this goal important?
  • What small action can I take today to move closer to this goal?
  • How will I measure success?
  • What obstacles might I encounter, and how can I address them?
  • Who can support me in this journey?

Define the Goal Clearly

Clearly define your goal to ensure that every small step taken is directed towards achieving a specific and meaningful outcome.

Reflect and Adjust Regularly

Set time aside for regular reflection and make adjustments as needed. Flexibility is key to staying on track and maintaining motivation.

Seek Support or Accountability

Find someone who can help keep you accountable, offer feedback, and provide encouragement along the way.

Stay Positive and Enjoy the Process

The journey towards improvement should be enjoyable. Embrace small wins, stay positive, and find joy in the process.
